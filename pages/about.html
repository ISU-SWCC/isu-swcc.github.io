Our History

Founded in the heart of Iowa's agricultural landscape, the Soil and Water Conservation Club (SWCC) at Iowa State University has a rich tradition of promoting sustainable land and water management practices. Since its inception, the club has served as a bridge between academic learning and professional application, bringing together students from diverse backgrounds who share a common goal: preserving our most vital natural resources.

The club's early years were marked by grassroots initiatives, student-led field trips to watershed management sites, and collaborative projects with local conservation districts. Over the decades, SWCC has grown into a premier student organization, recognized for its commitment to environmental stewardship and its contribution to the university's mission of practical, hands-on education.

Mission

Mission Statement

To foster a community of students dedicated to the advancement of soil and water conservation through education, professional development, and service-oriented projects that benefit both the university and the surrounding environment.

Vision

Vision Statement

To be a leading student chapter that inspires future generations of conservation professionals to implement innovative solutions for a sustainable and resilient agricultural future.
